1 Retention & Engagement

Historically the weakest section — fix this.

Q Why is DAU only a fraction of downloads? Why do users explore and drop off?

  • You're right — our DAU numbers are quite low vs. the install base. This is primarily because we're still shaping our product + technology. It's been just 6 months that we've been building the consumer interface, and we're doing it for the first time — so we're learning on the go.
  • I strongly believe our engagement rates will improve drastically when we move to Maya 3 — which will have way better latency, memory, and reasoning.
  • We'll also work toward better UX for our hero use cases (like shopping and other assistant tasks).

Q What do your daily users actually do?

  • Users who stick around primarily use Maya as a combination of assistant + companion.
  • Assistant (hero use cases): image generation, then product discovery for shopping, and searching for news and information.
  • Companionship: people talk about relationships, faith, and just generally chat about their day-to-day life.

2 Business Model & Monetization

Q Why are you doing enterprise? How will you maintain focus?

  • We believe enterprise will be critical to making the company a lot more resilient — for two reasons.
  • (1) It's not a drastic detour for us. The core of the enterprise solution is the same thing: the quality of our models. If you're building great models, it's relatively easy to package them and sell to enterprises, especially in the form of paid APIs.
  • (2) Monetization is more deterministic in enterprise than in consumer. The biggest proof: all the big labs do both — they definitely have an enterprise motion going, and some also do consumer. Enterprise is part of the playbook for every big tech lab.

Q What will be your enterprise GTM?

  • Fortunately, Maya has seen a lot of inbound interest. The model has been downloaded ~500K (5 lakh) times on Hugging Face, so there's deep awareness in the developer community — and we get inbounds from the developer, startup, and big-tech ecosystem.
  • (1) We'll cater to these inbound requests.
  • (2) We'll set up a self-serve motion for our paid APIs, banking on the popularity Maya already has in the developer ecosystem.

Q Will you also do managed and on-prem?

  • For now, no. First we'll go to market with paid APIs only, scale that, and then consider managed / on-prem.

3 Market & the "Rest of the World" Thesis

Q People who've tried apps in local Indian languages still prefer English. Why is this different?

  • Two parts. First, the previous generation of "local language" was built on inferior, broken technology — it was transliterating, not really translating. As a result users got a very subpar experience.
  • Second, and more importantly, language is just one part of the interface. The interface also includes the entire navigation — which button to click, which card to tap. Navigating all of those paths is beyond translation and is still tricky.
  • Only a voice + reasoning layer can navigate those elements of the interface — and that is possible only now, with the latest voice models.

Q How do you scale the India data-collection playbook globally?

  • India is a single country, but an extremely complex language market — each state has its own dialects, and there are tons of languages: ~30 languages with thousands of dialects.
  • Our belief: if we can navigate India, we can use the same playbook across the world — and that's held true so far. We're Telugu-native founders, yet we've cracked English, Bengali, and a few other major languages.
  • If we can crack those, we believe we can go beyond India and crack the languages of other countries too.

Q How big is this really? Quantify "the rest of the world."

  • We believe the entire world excluding English-speaking countries is the addressable "rest of the world."
  • That primarily means India, MENA, and other developing parts of the world.

★ Delivery reminders (cross-cutting, from the debriefs)

  • Open with the audio. Play the 3 comparison clips (Maya vs. others) before slides to anchor quality instantly. Don't name competitors — let the audience pick.
  • Bring the demo early, right after the audio anchor.
  • Lead with the "why." Don't wait to be asked why Maya is better — the cost hook ($1M vs $250M) and #4-global ranking go early.
  • One idea at a time. Finish a point before the next; the architecture slide reveals one quadrant at a time.
  • Bharath controls the mouse when he talks; agree a shared signal to hand energy back and forth.
  • Energy: read the room, take the audience with you. The conference pitch was the best version — confident and crisp; avoid going formal/rigid.
  • Pick one lane: a consumer company that happens to own frontier voice models. Not a split consumer/enterprise story.
